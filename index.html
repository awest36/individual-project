Artist Statement

As we are driven by a sense of identity, artists push their audience to recognize themselves in any form possible. Essentially, viewers define themselves internally with reference to the artist and the artist’s work. Artists utilize their work to impel us to recognize, “I remember, I have, I can, I am, and I will.” Through this recognition, the audience is able to recognize who they are within their role as the viewer, within their role as the potential artist, and also within their prominent and entitled role as an individual who leads beyond a limited perspective and strengthens the foundation of their knowledge through recognizing the depth and command of their visual language and its ability to instill meaning. Through this process, we merge the art with the human experience. This is where we recognize art as the human experience; this is where we make art the human experience. The implicit truth of art forces us to create a relationship- and this relationship is between the art and our interaction with it. Art changes us as we change art.
Meaning can never exist within the art. It exists only within two people: You and the Artist.
